# PSYCHOLOGY SKILL

Last updated: 2026-06-26
Version: 1
Scope: Content Writer, Copy Agent, Sales Agent, Marketing Agent

## PURPOSE

Apply proven psychological principles to every piece of
content and copy. Not manipulation — alignment with how
people actually make decisions.

## CORE PRINCIPLES

### 1. Loss Aversion

People are more motivated by what they might lose than
what they might gain.

Application:
  "Stop leaving money on the table" > "Make more money"
  "The mistake costing you clients" > "How to get clients"
  "You're probably doing this wrong" > "Do this right"

### 2. Social Proof

People follow the actions of others, especially peers.

Application:
  "Built 3 funnels this week for clients" — shows activity
  Specific numbers: "Generated $2,400 in 11 days" > "Made money"
  Client results with details, not generic testimonials

### 3. Scarcity / Urgency

Things become more valuable when they feel limited.

Application:
  Time-based: "Before Friday" not "soon"
  Access-based: "Only taking 3 clients this month"
  Result-based: "First 5 people get X"

### 4. Authority

People defer to experts. Position as the person who
has done the thing, not just studied it.

Application:
  Lead with results, not credentials
  "I built X and here's what happened" > "I'm an expert in X"
  Show the work, not the theory

### 5. Commitment / Consistency

Small yeses lead to big yeses. Get micro-commitments first.

Application:
  Free value → paid offer
  "Try this one thing" → "Now try this bigger thing"
  Content builds familiarity before the pitch

### 6. Anchoring

The first number someone sees becomes the reference point.

Application:
  "$500/month" before "$200/month" makes $200 feel cheap
  Show the expensive option first in any comparison
  Frame savings as percentages AND dollars

## HOOK FRAMEWORKS

### Problem-Agitate-Solve (PAS)

Line 1: State the problem clearly
Line 2: Agitate — show why it's worse than they think
Line 3: Solve — present the fix

### Before-After-Bridge (BAB)

Line 1: Here's where you are now (before)
Line 2: Here's where you could be (after)
Line 3: Here's how to get there (bridge)

### Attention-Interest-Desire-Action (AIDA)

Line 1: Grab attention (pattern interrupt)
Line 2: Build interest (specific detail)
Line 3: Create desire (benefit or result)
Line 4: Drive action (clear CTA)

## ETHICAL GUARDRAILS

- Never fabricate results or numbers
- Never imply false urgency (fake deadlines)
- Never use fear to manipulate — use it to illuminate
- Always deliver on the promise the hook makes
- Social proof must be real or clearly labeled as example
