# ATTENTION SKILL

Last updated: 2026-06-26
Version: 1
Scope: Content Writer, Editorial Agent, Copy Agent

## PURPOSE

Every piece of content must stop the scroll. The first
line, first image, first frame is the only thing that
matters if nobody reads past it.

## THE 3-SECOND RULE

You have 3 seconds before someone scrolls past.
Those 3 seconds are won or lost by:

1. The hook (first line of text)
2. The visual (first image or frame)
3. The pattern interrupt (something unexpected)

## HOOK TYPES

### The Contrarian

State the opposite of what people believe.

  "Posting daily is killing your reach."
  "Your funnel doesn't need more traffic."
  "I stopped tracking followers. Revenue doubled."

### The Specific Number

Exact numbers catch attention because they imply proof.

  "I made $2,400 in 11 days with one carousel."
  "3 clients in 48 hours. Here's the DM script."
  "Lost 847 followers in a month. Best thing that happened."

### The Open Loop

Start a story or claim. Don't finish it yet.

  "I was about to shut down my SaaS. Then I changed one thing on the pricing page."
  "A client asked me why their leads dropped 60%. The answer surprised me."

### The Direct Call-Out

Name the exact person you're talking to.

  "SaaS founders charging under $50/month: read this."
  "If you run a newsletter and haven't added a second revenue stream..."
  "Creators with 10K+ followers making $0 from content."

### The Visual Pattern Interrupt

Something that looks different from everything else on the feed.

  Dark background with one orange accent (brand consistency)
  Carousel that starts with a bold stat, not a title
  Image with text overlay that raises a question

## PATTERN INTERRUPTS FOR EACH PLATFORM

LinkedIn:
  - Single line paragraph followed by whitespace
  - "Unpopular opinion:" then the take
  - Numbered list that subverts expectations

X/Twitter:
  - First tweet is controversial or specific
  - Thread opener that promises a result, not a lesson
  - Image tweet with minimal text

Instagram:
  - Carousel slide 1: bold claim or question on dark background
  - Reel hook: motion + text in first 1 second
  - Story: poll or question sticker immediately

Facebook:
  - Image-first posts with short caption
  - "Story" format: "Yesterday I [did X]. Here's what happened."
  - Question that invites comments

## WHAT KILLS ATTENTION

- Starting with "Hey guys" or "Happy Monday"
- Generic advice that could apply to anyone
- Long preambles before getting to the point
- Stock photos or generic graphics
- Posts that look like every other post in the feed
- Hedging: "I think maybe this could potentially help..."
