# SALES SKILL

Last updated: 2026-06-26
Version: 1
Scope: Sales Agent, Copy Agent, Client Agent

## PURPOSE

Turn attention into revenue. Every sales interaction must
feel like a conversation, not a pitch. The goal is to
help someone decide if what you offer is right for them.

## THE SALES FRAMEWORK

### Step 1: Identify the Pain

Before selling anything, understand:
  - What is the person struggling with?
  - How long have they been struggling?
  - What have they already tried?
  - What is the cost of not fixing this?

Ask questions. Listen. Do not pitch until you understand.

### Step 2: Quantify the Loss

Help them see what inaction costs:

  "You're losing approximately $X per month by not fixing Y."
  "Every week this goes unresolved, you're leaving Z on the table."
  "The gap between where you are and where you could be is $X."

Numbers make pain tangible. Vague pain gets ignored.

### Step 3: Present the Fix

Describe the solution in terms of:
  - What changes (specific)
  - What the result looks like (visual)
  - How long it takes (timeline)
  - What they need to do (effort)

Do not describe features. Describe outcomes.

### Step 4: Remove Risk

Make it easy to say yes:
  - Guarantee or revision policy
  - Small entry point (audit, call, assessment)
  - Social proof from similar clients
  - Clear timeline for results

### Step 5: Close with Clarity

  "Here's what I'm proposing: [specific deliverable]"
  "The investment is: [price]"
  "Here's what happens next: [immediate step]"
  "I need from you: [specific thing]"

No ambiguity. No "let me know." Clear next step.

## OBJECTION HANDLING

### "Too expensive"

  Reframe: "Compared to what? The cost of not fixing this is $X/month."
  Or: "What's your budget? I can scope something that fits."

### "I need to think about it"

  Respect it, but clarify: "What specifically do you want to think through?"
  Often reveals the real objection.

### "I've tried before and it didn't work"

  Acknowledge: "What went wrong last time?"
  Differentiate: "Here's how our approach is different."

### "Can you do it cheaper?"

  Do not discount immediately. Ask: "What's your budget?"
  If you can scope down, do. If not, hold the price.

## DM OUTREACH RULES

- Never open with a pitch
- Reference something specific they posted or did
- Offer value first (insight, resource, observation)
- Ask if they're open to a conversation
- Never follow up more than 2 times without a response

## EMAIL SEQUENCES

Cold email structure:
  Line 1: Specific observation about their business
  Line 2: The problem you see
  Line 3: What you've done for similar businesses
  Line 4: Soft CTA (question, not pitch)

Follow-up rhythm:
  Day 0: Initial email
  Day 3: Follow-up with additional value
  Day 7: Final follow-up with clear CTA
  Stop: If no response after 3 touches

## CONVERSION PRINCIPLES

1. Specificity beats generality: "$2,400 in 11 days" > "made money"
2. Social proof beats claims: show, don't tell
3. Risk reversal beats discounts: guarantee > lower price
4. Urgency must be real: fake deadlines destroy trust
5. The close is a service: helping them decide, not pressuring
