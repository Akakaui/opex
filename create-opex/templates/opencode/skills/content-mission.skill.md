# CONTENT MISSION SKILL

Last updated: 2026-06-26
Version: 1
Scope: Content Planner, Content Writer, Editorial Agent

## PURPOSE

Every piece of content must have a mission. Nothing is
created "just to post." Before any content is written,
the Content Planner must assign a mission.

## THE 5 MISSIONS

Every piece of content serves exactly one of these:

### 1. AUTHORITY

Goal: Establish expertise and credibility.
Signal: "This person knows what they're talking about."

Content types:
  - Case studies with specific numbers
  - Framework explanations with examples
  - "Here's how I solved X" posts
  - Technical deep dives

KPI: Saves, shares, profile visits

### 2. AWARENESS

Goal: Reach new people who don't know you yet.
Signal: "Who is this? I should follow."

Content types:
  - Hot takes and opinions
  - Contrarian viewpoints
  - Trend commentary
  - Relatable observations

KPI: Impressions, reach, new followers

### 3. CONNECTION

Goal: Build relationship and trust with existing audience.
Signal: "I feel like I know this person."

Content types:
  - Behind-the-scenes
  - Personal stories and lessons
  - Failures and what was learned
  - Day-in-the-life content

KPI: Comments, DMs, replies

### 4. ACTIVATION

Goal: Move audience toward a specific action.
Signal: "I should check this out / sign up / buy."

Content types:
  - Offer announcements
  - Testimonials with results
  - "Here's what I'm launching" posts
  - Free resource → paid upgrade

KPI: Clicks, sign-ups, purchases

### 5. PROOF

Goal: Show evidence that what you do works.
Signal: "This actually delivers results."

Content types:
  - Client results (with permission)
  - Revenue screenshots
  - Before/after comparisons
  - Process reveals

KPI: DMs, replies, saves

## MISSION ALLOCATION RULE

Not every mission gets equal time. Recommended weekly split:

  Authority:   2 posts (40%)
  Awareness:   1 post (20%)
  Connection:  1 post (20%)
  Activation:  1 post (10%)
  Proof:       1 post (10%)

Total: 5 posts minimum per week (adjust per platform)

## BEFORE WRITING CHECKLIST

Content Planner must answer these before assigning content:

1. Which mission does this serve?
2. Who is the specific audience for this piece?
3. What should the reader DO after consuming this?
4. Which platform is this optimized for?
5. What's the hook that stops the scroll?
6. How does this connect to an active goal?

If any answer is "I don't know" → stop. Revisit strategy.
